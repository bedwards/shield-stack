1. GhostBoard — Employer Ghosting Rate Database
The gripe: 80% of job applicants get ghosted. 75% of applications get zero response. One job hunter applied to 1,782 jobs over 14 months — 1,400 rejections and 200+ ghosting incidents. The r/jobs subreddit (3.5M members) is consumed by this. Congress published a CRS report on "ghost job postings." Lawmakers introduced legislation in August 2025 to end HR ghosting.

Why no competitor exists: Glassdoor has interview reviews but no "ghosting rate" metric. The Interview Guys' Ghosting Index gives industry-level data but not per-company. Jobright publishes aggregate reports but no searchable database. No app lets you type "Company X" and see "this company ghosts 73% of applicants."

The product: Search any company, see its ghosting rate (% of applicants who never hear back), average response time, and interview-to-offer ratio. Crowdsourced from job seekers who report outcomes. Free to search, premium for alerts and analytics.

Revenue model: Freemium + job board affiliate commissions. Recruiters pay to see their company's score and improve it.

SEO play: "Does [company name] ghost applicants" — millions of monthly searches across thousands of companies.

Wave: 2026 job market frustration. Legislative momentum. Remote work = more applications per person = more ghosting.

Sources: The 2025 Ghosting Index, Jobright 2025 Ghosted Jobs Report, CNBC: Lawmakers want to end HR ghosting

2. CliffCheck — All-Program, All-State Benefits Cliff Calculator
The gripe: A SNAP household of four earning $3,348/month gets $500 in SNAP benefits. Earn $500 more and you lose ALL $500 in SNAP. Workers report being "forced to reduce hours to stay on Medicaid." Crisis Assistance Ministry (Feb 2026) calls it "when progress costs too much."

Why no competitor exists: APHSA's dashboard covers only 12 states. Federal Reserve Bank of Atlanta covers only their district. Individual program calculators (SNAP, Medicaid, EITC) exist but are siloed. No tool shows the COMBINED cliff across ALL programs simultaneously for ANY state.

The product: Enter your income, household size, state. See a single graph showing what happens to your total benefits (SNAP + Medicaid + EITC + childcare subsidies + housing + LIHEAP + TANF) as your income rises. "Earn $1 more and lose $X" — visualized plainly.

Revenue model: Ad-supported (high-intent visitors for credit unions, benefits counseling, workforce programs). Affiliate partnerships with financial literacy organizations.

SEO play: "Will I lose my benefits if I get a raise" — searched by millions of Americans in benefits programs.

Wave: 2025 SNAP work requirement expansion. ACA subsidy expiration in 2026 (premiums doubling for 20M people). Benefits cliffs are steeper than ever.

Sources: APHSA Benefit Cliff Dashboard, Crisis Assistance Ministry, US Chamber Foundation

The product: Take a 5-question quiz (score drop amount, loan balance, current income, life goals). Get a personalized recovery plan: IBR enrollment, rehabilitation program, or consolidation — ranked by timeline to score recovery and total cost. Track progress over time.

Revenue model: Affiliate commissions (credit-builder products, refinancing when eligible, credit monitoring services). All free to user.

SEO play: "Student loan credit score dropped what to do" — 2.2M people Googling this right now.

Wave: This crisis is happening RIGHT NOW in March 2026. Time-sensitive. First-mover advantage is huge.

Sources: NY Fed: Student Loan Delinquencies, Sacramento Observer, CBS News

Revenue model: Freemium (free scans, premium for household inventory + push alerts at $2.99/month). Ad-supported free tier.

The gripe: Shady repairmen recommend $400 compressor replacements when the fix is a dirty $0 coil cleaning. NerdWallet covered Reddit threads about the repair-vs-replace dilemma. If quoted above $200, get a second opinion."

Revenue model: Free lookups (ad-supported), premium for detailed reports ($2.99 each). Affiliate with vetted repair services.

SEO play: "How much should [appliance] [repair type] cost" — extremely high intent, high volume.

Wave: Tariffs increasing appliance replacement costs → more people repairing → more need for fair pricing info.

Revenue model: $19.99 per full background check review, or $9.99/month for monitoring. Identity protection upsell.

SEO play: "Background check wrong information" / "how to dispute background check error" — high intent, desperate audience.

Wave: Gig economy growth = more background checks. AI-driven screening increasing error rates. CFPB scrutiny.

Sources: FTC: Disputing Background Check Errors, Privacy Rights Clearinghouse, CFPB on Background Check Inaccuracies
